You want to maintain order and principles. If that does not work, you want to make a choice depending on what's more reasonable in the situation: to give in with conditions or to forcefully engage.

Your Motives & Values (Blue-Green MVS)

As a person with a Blue-Green MVS, you achieve feelings of self-worth by being genuinely helpful to others while developing self-sufficiency for yourself and others.

You assess other people's needs and contribute to their self-reliance. You assist people who are genuinely in need of help and are willing to use the help offered. You move quietly to the aid of others with an efficient plan. You believe in prudent helpfulness and set limits on the assistance you give to others.

You create fair processes that help people become self-reliant. For you, the real measure of success is how helpful you can be without diminishing the independence of those you are helping. You balance principles and feelings, logic and emotion.

How You Experience Conflict (G-[BR])

You first seek information or rational explanations. You analyze the situation and focus on facts. You are cautious and hesitant to prevent making a mistake. You believe that the best way to show you care about the problem is to give it the time and attention it needs.

You want people to remain objective and give you some time to think. You do not want people to be confrontational, overly emotional, or make hasty decisions.

If conflict progresses to Stage 2, you may accommodate with conditions or assert your rights. At Stage 3, you feel an urgent need to end it and will either fight or give up.

Path back to Blue-Green MVS: Creating processes that help others help themselves.

Top 3 Strengths at Work
Cautious

You are a cautious decision maker, patiently thinking through matters before taking action. You spot potential problems, challenges, or traps before others do — helping yourself and others avoid costly mistakes.

Analytical

You synthesize information in a rational way and make logical connections for others that create "aha moments." You discover patterns and underlying causes that others have not found.

Option-Oriented

You find your sweet spot in coming up with alternatives that others haven't considered. You suggest different ways of doing things and offer an incredible fusion of new perspectives.

Top 3 Overdone Strengths
Supportive
Self-Sacrificing

You tend to put other people's interests ahead of yours. When others don't appreciate how much you've invested, you can become resentful. Place reasonable limits on the support you provide to avoid depleting yourself.

Analytical
Obsessed

When things don't make logical sense, you can get obsessed with finding an explanation — and freeze. Key checks: What if there is no answer? What is the simplest way to explain this? Is this worth my time?

Helpful
Smothering

You can get frustrated when people won't let you help them. Sometimes the best way to help someone is to let them take care of matters on their own and remain available only when asked.

How to Communicate with Yani
When things are going well
  • Ask their opinion before sharing your own; offer logical proposals without pushing too assertively.
  • Emphasize principles and fairness.
  • Recognize how the process will impact people.
When there is conflict
  • Respect their need for time to think things through.
  • Keep calm, unemotional, and state ideas in logical order.
  • Focus on getting things "right."
